## 1. Problem (recon-grounded, probe-verified)

`restore --md1` routes ALL `Tag::Tr` md1 through `restore.rs::taproot_template_and_internal_key` (`:675`), which reconstructs ONLY single-leaf NUMS `multi_a`/`sortedmulti_a` (`:703-713`) and refuses everything else ("not a recognized multisig", `:710`). But md-codec renders general `tr(NUMS, <miniscript>)` leaves AND depth-1 two-leaf `tr(NUMS,{a,b})` correctly, and they Display+reparse cleanly on the toolkit's pinned miniscript `95fdd1c` (probe-confirmed). `faithful_multisig_descriptor` (`:911`) — the general-policy reconstruction arm — has NO `Tag::Tr` guard; it's "non-taproot-only" purely because §3 intercepts tr first. So the Display-safe subset is faithfully restorable NOW with zero md-codec change.

**Two probe findings that shape the design:**
- **The `ReconstructTranslator::pk` (`:856-863`) rejects the NUMS `Single` key** — every `tr(NUMS,…)` rendering carries exactly 1 `DescriptorPublicKey::Single(XOnly(H-point))`; naive routing to the faithful arm FAILS at `translate_pk`. T3 MUST add a `Single` pass-through.
- **The `95fdd1c` Display bug is LEFT-branch-specific, not depth-specific** (a `TapTree` as a LEFT child mis-Displays `{{a,b,c}}`; right-spine of any depth Displays fine). The conservative **≤2-leaf gate (no nested `TapTree`)** is the right contract anyway (a contract keyed to "shapes a known upstream bug happens to spare" is fragile + dissolves on the #953 release).

3. **Depth gate helper** — `fn taptree_depth_le_one(inner: &Node) -> bool` = "no `Tag::TapTree` is the child of a `Tag::TapTree`" (md-codec taptrees are strictly binary ⇒ ≤2 leaves ⟺ depth ≤1 ⟺ no nested TapTree).
